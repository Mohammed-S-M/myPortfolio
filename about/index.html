About Me

I was born and raised in Baghdad, Iraq. I lived my childhood and teenage years in my country until I became 18, then moved into a neighboring country. After a few years, I moved into the United States of America when I was 21 years old.


Since I was a child, I have loved computers and have always wondered how this incredible machine works in hardware and software. It was magical for me. Looking at the screen makes me happy as I keep wondering how this thing works. When I became a teenager, my parents bought me my first computer, and that was the starting point of experiencing what was that magical machine all about. I started to experiment on the software part and the hardware. Start to learn how to format the PC and install the Windows operating system. Experimenting on many software applications from Photoshop to Microsoft office, it was a world of unlimited possibilities. Also, I used to save all the money my parents used to give me to upgrade my hardware. In other words, the computer was my life and still.


However, since I came to the US, the world of possibilities was open for me to learn how this remarkable machine works, so I decided to pursue a computer science major. Since I started learning about computers in deep learning, I realized my love for coding started growing as I learned Java, C, and C++. However, that was not enough for me, and I wanted to see every possible coding path to choose where I wanted to be. After deep searching online, I fell in love with web development, especially the front-end part. So I decided to be a self-learner in the learning path to be a front-end web developer, and I love every step of the way. Web development is a vast world, and there are no limits to learning in this field.
